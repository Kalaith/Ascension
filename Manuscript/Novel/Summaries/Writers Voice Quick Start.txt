Writers Voice Quick Start - Ascension

Use this when drafting a new chapter fast.

Voice in 5 Rules
- Write in close third person (usually Ksena or Alcuin).
- Keep language direct, emotional, and clear.
- Mix personal stakes with larger conflict.
- Use dialogue often to move plot and relationships.
- End with a hook (new risk, reveal, or decision).

Scene Formula
1. Immediate objective (what they need now).
2. Complication (threat, disagreement, timing pressure).
3. Action/dialogue movement.
4. Reveal or shift (lore, emotion, strategy, power).
5. Decision and forward hook.

Character Anchors
- Ksena: determined, empathetic, curious, emotionally open.
- Alcuin: protective, practical, loyal, grounded.
- Elders/grandmother: mission-focused, strategic, history-aware.
- Humans/Kirk: external perspective, tech contrast, alliance pressure.

World Anchors to Reuse
- Ascension and power cost.
- Crystal/control system.
- City barrier and politics.
- Temple/ancient truth.
- Tribe vs city cultural tension.

Dialogue Checklist
- Keep lines plain and character-driven.
- Add short physical beats between lines.
- Use tension + warmth (conflict plus care/banter).

Do Not
- Do not over-explain lore in one block.
- Do not drift into detached narration.
- Do not resolve big conflict without cost.
- Do not end scenes flat.

Final Pass Checklist
- POV consistent?
- One relationship beat included?
- One plot progression beat included?
- Lore consistent with established rules?
- Ending pulls into next chapter?
